Altitude Adjustment

You're at 7,000 feet! Here's what that means:

🏔️ High Altitude Tips
  • Drink extra water — you'll dehydrate faster up here
  • Alcohol hits harder — go easy on the first night
  • You may feel winded on hikes — take it slow at first
  • Sunscreen is essential — UV is stronger at elevation
  • Water boils at ~198°F — pasta and rice take longer to cook

Best Time to Visit Attractions

  • Grand Canyon: Arrive before 9 AM to beat crowds and parking issues
  • Sedona: Weekdays are much less crowded than weekends
  • Snowbowl: Midweek skiing is the sweet spot; weekends get packed
  • Lowell Observatory: Book stargazing programs in advance — they sell out

Weather Tips

  • Summer (Jun-Aug): Warm days, cool nights, afternoon monsoon thunderstorms (usually 2-4 PM). They pass quickly!
  • Fall (Sep-Nov): Gorgeous colors, crisp air. Aspens peak in late September/early October.
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): Snow is common and beautiful. Roads can close — check conditions before driving.
  • Spring (Mar-May): Unpredictable — can be warm one day, snowy the next. Layers are key.

Getting the Best Hot Tub Experience

  • Go at night — Flagstaff has some of the darkest skies in the country. You'll see the Milky Way!
  • Winter soaks are incredible — steam rising into the cold pine air
  • Leave the cover off while you soak but put it back on when done

Local Secrets

  • Route 66 runs right through downtown Flagstaff — cruise it at sunset
  • The Flagstaff Farmer's Market (Sundays in summer) is fantastic
  • Heritage Square downtown often has free live music on weekends
  • The best sunsets are from Mars Hill (Lowell Observatory area)
  • Elk sightings are most common at dawn and dusk — look along I-17 and side roads

Money-Saving Tips

  • Sedona is expensive — eat in Flagstaff before or after your day trip
  • America the Beautiful Pass ($80/year) covers Grand Canyon, Walnut Canyon, Sunset Crater, and more
  • Happy hours at Flagstaff breweries are a great deal
  • Pack a picnic for day trips — scenic pulloffs are everywhere
